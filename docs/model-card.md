# Model Card — Diabetes Risk Predictor

> Following the Mitchell et al. "Model Cards for Model Reporting" template.

## Model details
- **Author:** Tadaishe Maumbe
- **Date:** 2026
- **Version:** 0.1.0
- **Type:** Binary classifier (HistGradientBoosting wrapped in a scikit-learn pipeline with median imputation + standard scaling)
- **License:** MIT

## Intended use
- **Primary use:** Pre-test triage at primary-care visits — flagging patients who should receive a confirmatory HbA1c.
- **Primary users:** Clinicians, community-health workers, public-health researchers.
- **Out of scope:** Clinical diagnosis; insurance underwriting; any use without clinician oversight.

## Factors
- Trained on females, ≥21 y, of Pima heritage. Performance on males, other ethnic groups, or paediatric populations is **not validated** and likely degrades.
- All measurements assumed to come from standard clinical-laboratory procedures matching the original NIDDK protocol.

## Metrics
Headline metrics are reported on a held-out 20% stratified test set. See `models/metrics.json` (generated by `python -m diabetes_mlops.train`) for the exact values for your run, and `mlruns/` for full per-experiment detail.

## Training data
- Pima Indians Diabetes Database (768 rows, 8 features), via the jbrownlee/Datasets public mirror.
- Cleaning step replaces physiologically impossible zeros with NaN before model-pipeline imputation.

## Evaluation data
- 20% stratified split of the same dataset, untouched until model selection is complete.

## Ethical considerations
- **Single-population training set** ⇒ deploying this model on a different demographic without re-validation is unsafe and could amplify health-equity gaps.
- **Asymmetric error cost** — false negatives (missed diabetics) are clinically worse than false positives. The decision threshold is tuned to bias the model toward over-referral, not balanced accuracy.
- **No PHI handled** — the model takes anonymous numeric inputs only; the API does not log payloads by default.

## Caveats and recommendations
- Re-train at least annually as patient demographics drift.
- Re-validate before any deployment to a new clinical setting.
- Pair the prediction with the SHAP-driven explanation in the Streamlit app — opaque scores erode clinician trust.
